Pepper Mill

This course provides an introduction to the ideas and techniques of Industrial Design and is intended for students from engineering, design, or business with an interest in multi-disciplinary, needs-based product design methods. It will follow a workshop model, combining weekly lectures on industrial design history, processes, tools, and the profession, with a progressive set of studio-based design projects. It includes overviews of materials/manufacturing, design research, human factors/ergonomics, sustainability, and visual design and communication. This course will not enable one to become an industrial designer but will enable one to understand and appreciate what industrial design does, what it can contribute to society and why it can be so rewarding.

Goals & Objectives:
Learn how Industrial designers operate and how they approach a project
Overview of problem-solving techniques used by industrial designers
Overview of the history of industrial design
Industrial design communication and visualization techniques (sketching and prototyping)
